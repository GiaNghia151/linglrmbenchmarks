I'll help solve Problem 1 about Yonggom language first, as this appears to be the most straightforward to analyze systematically.

Let's analyze the patterns in the Yonggom sentences:

1) The verb endings show different patterns for different subjects:
- -aan/-een (I/he/she)
- -iib/-eeb (you sg/they)
- -uub/-anuub (we)

2) When actions are connected:
- -e/-u + another verb = same subject
- -a/-oona + another verb = different subject

3) Key vocabulary:
- om = bread
- ok/oka = water  
- anon = dog
- awon = pig
- oon = fish
- karub = man
- weng = voice/sound
- ambib = house

4) Common verbs:
- an- = eat
- ban-/ben- = take
- wedm- = see
- wingk- = sing
- yeed- = get up
- wen- = go away
- min- = come
- wengambar- = hear
