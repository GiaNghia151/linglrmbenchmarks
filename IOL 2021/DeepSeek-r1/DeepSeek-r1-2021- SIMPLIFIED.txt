Problem 1 Solutions

(a) Numerals in digits:

	•	moeto waroewo — 138
	•	ijema joka rati — 39
	•	moeto wido ma benomi ka wi — 114
	•	One of these numerals matches an existing example: "ijema joka rati" corresponds to 39, matching "ije mepija" = 39.

(b) Write out in Ekari:

	1.	1 — ije
	2.	19 — benomima rati idibi or mepija ka ije
	3.	26 — waroewoma rati joka
	4.	104 — moeto wija wija
	5.	292 — moeto idibi ma mepina daemita moeto idibi
	•	The number that can be expressed two ways is 1, which can be written as "ije" or possibly "ena".

Problem 3 Solutions

(a) Translations into English:

	14.	Navila vivila biyamata tomwaya mtona? — How many women will look after this old man?
	15.	Bikamkwamsi kweyu vivila minasina. — They will eat those four women.
	16.	Amagudina gwadi lekota? — Which child arrived?
	17.	Tevila tauwau bigisesi gugwadi gudigasisi? — How many men will see those clever children?
	18.	Legisesi ketala waga vivila minasiwena. — The women saw those two canoes.
	•	Two possible translations for Sentence 15: They will eat those four women. / Those four women will eat something.

(b) Translations into Kilivila:

	19.	How many canoes did those old women see? — Navila waga legisesi nunumwaya minasiwena?
	20.	These four white men will look after this clever child. — Biyamatasi dimdim minasina tauwau gwadi magudiwena nakabitam.
	21.	How many children will eat these pigs? — Navila gwadi bikamkwamsi bunukwa minana.
	22.	Which woman caught those beautiful fish? — Amtona vivila lekalimati tetala minawena gudimanabweta?
	23.	Two wild dogs saw that old man. — Legisi ka’ukwa minasiwena tau magudiwena.
