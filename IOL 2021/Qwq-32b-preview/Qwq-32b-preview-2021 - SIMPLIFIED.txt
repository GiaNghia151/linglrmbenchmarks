Problem 1 Solutions

(a) Numerals in digits:

	•	moeto waroewo — 130
		•	(moeto=100 + waroewo=30).
		•	waroewo is derived from waroewoma (30) minus the suffix -ma, which may indicate a decrement of 10 (30 → 20? Not consistent, but based on examples, assume waroewo = 30).
	•	ijema joka rati — 38
		•	(ije=30 + joka=2 + rati=6).
		•	This matches the value of waroewoma joka rati (38), so this is the duplicate.
	•	moeto wido ma ije ka wi — 129
		•	(moeto=100 + wido=29).
		•	This is the same as the given example moeto wija ma ije ka wido, so this is the duplicate.

(b) Write out in Ekari:

	1.	1 — wi
	2.	19 — benomima mepija
	3.	26 — waroewo rati
	4.	104 — moeto beo
	5.	292 — moeto idibi ma widoma mepija ka benomi
	•	1 — (since wija = 2, so wi likely means 1).
	•	19 — (Two possible expressions for 19: benomima mepija (benomima=10 + mepija=9) and enama mepija (enama=10 + mepija=9), but enama is 10 (from enama rati beo = 11). Thus, 19 has two forms: benomima mepija and enama mepija).
	•	26 — (waroewo=20 + rati=6).
	•	104 — (moeto=100 + beo=4). beo likely represents 4 (from idibima rati beo = 55: 50 + 5 = 55, so beo may mean 0 or a modifier; here, assume beo = 4 for consistency).
	•	292 — (same as the given example for 343, but adjusted for 292). Alternative: moeto idibi beo benomima (assuming idibi = 200, beo = subtract 50, but this is speculative). The problem states one number can be expressed two ways. 19 could also be enama mepija (enama=10 + mepija=9), but enama is 10 (from enama rati beo = 11). Thus, 19 has two forms: benomima mepija and enama mepija.
