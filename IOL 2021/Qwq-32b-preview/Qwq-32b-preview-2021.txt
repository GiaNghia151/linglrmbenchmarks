Problem 1 Solutions: 

(a) Numerals in Digits:  

    moeto waroewo    
        130  (moeto=100 + waroewo=30).  
        waroewo  is derived from waroewoma  (30) minus the suffix -ma , which may indicate a decrement of 10 (30 → 20? Not consistent, but based on examples, assume waroewo = 30 ).
         

    ijema joka rati    
        38  (ije=30 + joka=2 + rati=6).  
        This matches the value of waroewoma joka rati  (38), so this is the duplicate.
         

    moeto wido ma ije ka wi    
        129  (moeto=100 + wido=29).  
        This is the same as the given example moeto wija ma ije ka wido , so this is the duplicate.
         
     

(b) Numerals in Ekari:  

    1 : wi  (since wija = 2 , so wi  likely means 1).  
    19 : benomima mepija  (benomima=10 + mepija=9).  
    26 : waroewo rati  (waroewo=20 + rati=6).  
    104 : moeto beo  (moeto=100 + beo=4).  
        beo  likely represents 4 (from idibima rati beo = 55 : 50 + 5 = 55, so beo  may mean 0 or a modifier; here, assume beo = 4  for consistency).
         
    292 : moeto idibi ma widoma mepija ka benomi  (same as the given example for 343, but adjusted for 292).  
        Alternative : moeto idibi beo benomima  (assuming idibi = 200 , beo = subtract 50 , but this is speculative).  
        The problem states one number can be expressed two ways. 19  could also be enama mepija  (enama=10 + mepija=9), but enama  is 10 (from enama rati beo = 11 ). Thus, 19  has two forms: benomima mepija  and enama mepija .

Problem 4 Solutions (Agbirigba): 

(a) Exceptional Word : àtʊ́wà (to break) .   

    The rule is inserting t  + vowel between syllables. àtʊ́wà  breaks this pattern (no vowel after t ).  
    Correct form: àtɪ́wà .
     

(b) Agbirigba Forms:    

    rʊ́tʊ̂  → tɪŕʊ̂tʊ̂   
    rɛẁʊ̃́  → tɪrɛ̃̂wʊ̃̀   
    bіḱó  → tɪbіtḱó   
    ɲ́yẽ́ rɪỳã̀  → tɪɲ́yẽ̃́ tɪrɪỳã̀
