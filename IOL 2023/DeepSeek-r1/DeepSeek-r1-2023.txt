Problem 1 (a)

Answers for rows 15–18:
15. ɨnnetk'a
16. ɨŋɡɨrɨ'ɨ
17. ɨmp'uhur'u
18. ɨnher'o

Explanation:

    Prefix: Form 2 verbs start with ɨN-, where N assimilates to the place of articulation of Form 1’s initial consonant:

        Labials (e.g., p, p') → ɨm-

        Velars (e.g., k, k') → ɨŋ-

        Alveolars/nasals (e.g., t, n, h) → ɨn-

    Stem modification: The root undergoes metathesis, infixation, or vowel changes (e.g., piriy' → bir'i, tundiy' → dunat'i).

    Suffix: Form 1’s -y' often becomes -'i or -'ɨ, and -ay' becomes -a'a.

41. aplaʔ → It is being opened

46. p'ɨhnaʔ → I leap
47. ɨnhɨk'aʔ → It is being sewn, woven
48. ɨnipal'aʔ → I was bathed

Patterns:

    Pronouns:

        ɨm-, ɨn-, ɨŋ- prefixes mark 1st/2nd person.

        a-, e-, i- prefixes mark 3rd person or passive.

    Aspect:

        -aʔ = completive (past).

        -taʔ = inchoative (e.g., lie down).

        -paʔ = ongoing action (e.g., leave).
